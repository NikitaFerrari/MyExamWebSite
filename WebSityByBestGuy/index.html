Water

Water is a chemical substance that is a binary inorganic compound. A water molecule consists of two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom connected by a covalent bond. In 2020, an international team of scientists proved that water can exist as two liquids of different densities. Under certain conditions, they do not mix with each other. Water Resources Day is celebrated all over the world on March 22. Water is the simplest and most familiar substance on the planet. But at the same time, water is fraught with many mysteries. Scientists are still continuing to explore it, finding more and more interesting data about water. Water is the basis of life on Earth. It is indispensable for all living creatures and plays a key role in ecosystems, but much more is known about water than we can imagine. Water can remain liquid at temperatures below 0 °C. This property is endowed with ultrapure water without impurities (it is around them that crystallization forms). Even at -40 °C, it does not turn into ice and becomes supercooled.
